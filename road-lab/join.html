Join the Lab

We're seeking passionate researchers at all levels to join our community and contribute to advancing our understanding of adult development.

Research Opportunities

Undergraduate Research Assistant

Time Commitment: 8–15 hours/week (academic year or summer)

Undergraduate students at Brooklyn College are welcomed to join the lab. You'll get hands-on experience with data collection, data management, qualitative coding, literature review, and research design. Prior research experience is not required—just curiosity and a willingness to learn. You can earn course credit or participate through the Work-Study program.

  • Data collection and participant recruitment
  • Database management and analysis
  • Qualitative coding and thematic analysis
  • Literature reviews and research summaries
  • Possible co-authorship on publications

Master's Student (MA/MS)

Program: Psychology MA or MS

If you're a Brooklyn College or CUNY graduate student, Dr. Shane welcomes mentees in his lab. Master's students take a lead role in research design, data analysis, and manuscript development. You'll have the opportunity to develop your own research projects while contributing to ongoing lab work, and to present your findings at regional and national conferences.

  • Design and conduct independent research projects
  • Lead data analysis and statistical modeling
  • Co-author peer-reviewed publications
  • Present at conferences
  • Develop professional research skills

Doctoral Student (Ph.D.)

Program: CUNY Graduate Center Psychology Doctoral Program

If you're interested in pursuing a Ph.D. in lifespan developmental psychology, motivation and goals, or social factors in development, Dr. Shane is actively recruiting doctoral advisees through the CUNY Graduate Center. Ph.D. students in the lab develop an independent research agenda within our lab's broad framework, lead multiple studies, and prepare for careers in academic or applied research.

  • Develop and lead your own research agenda
  • Design and implement longitudinal and experimental studies
  • Lead publication development and dissemination
  • Mentor undergraduate and master's students
  • Present at major national conferences

What We Look For

Curiosity and Commitment

We value students who are genuinely curious about human behavior and development, who ask thoughtful questions, and who are willing to engage deeply with theory and evidence. You don't need prior research experience, but you do need intellectual curiosity and a willingness to learn.

Diverse Backgrounds

We actively recruit and support students from underrepresented backgrounds in psychology, including BIPOC, first-generation, LGBTQ+, and international students. We believe that diverse perspectives make our science stronger and our lab community richer. If you're wondering whether you belong here—you do.

Realistic Expectations

Research is rewarding but also challenging. We look for students who understand that science is iterative, that negative results teach us important lessons, and that setbacks are part of the process. We also value students who can communicate clearly, work collaboratively, and give and receive feedback gracefully.

Multi-Year Mentorship

Dr. Shane approaches mentorship as a multi-year commitment. Whether you're in the lab for one year or seven, he invests in your growth as a scholar, researcher, and person. He's available for regular meetings, wants to know about your career goals and challenges, and will help you develop the skills and knowledge you need for your next steps.

How to Apply

Undergraduate Researchers

Send an email to jshane@brooklyn.cuny.edu with:

  • A brief note (a few paragraphs) about why you're interested in the lab and what you hope to learn
  • Your current transcript (unofficial is fine)
  • Availability (expected hours/week and how many semesters)

We review applications on a rolling basis and respond within 1–2 weeks. New students can often start within 2–3 weeks of reaching out.

Master's Students

If you're a current or admitted MA/MS student at Brooklyn College or CUNY, reach out directly to Dr. Shane at jshane@brooklyn.cuny.edu. We can discuss your research interests and whether the lab is a good fit. You can also connect through the department graduate advisor if you need guidance on the process.

Doctoral Students

If you're applying to the CUNY Graduate Center Psychology Doctoral Program and are interested in working with Dr. Shane, mention this in your application. You can also reach out before applying to discuss potential research collaborations and whether we might be a good match. The formal admissions process is competitive but welcomes strong candidates from all backgrounds.

A note on mentorship: Dr. Shane mentors students across all three levels and deeply values long-term relationships with his advisees. Whether you're exploring the field as an undergrad or developing an independent research program as a doctoral student, he welcomes inquiries and conversations.

Ready to get started?

Send Dr. Shane an email telling him about your interests and what draws you to this work. We look forward to hearing from you.
